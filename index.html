Professional Instrument Rental Program

Background

Johnstonbaugh's Music Centers operates as a school music dealer and a retail music store, with its primary business centered on supplying musical instruments to school music programs and renting instruments to students. Much of the company's revenue comes from its beginner instrument rental program, where customers complete rental agreements via an online form hosted on its website. Administrative people then manually process these agreements through the company's Management Information System.

The professional instrument rental program existed before my hiring in 2021 as a Marketing and Website Specialist. However, it was only accessible within the beginner rental form, listed and categorized like another school, which lacked clarity or visibility for users. The user journey for this program was convoluted, requiring step-by-step instructions at the beginning of the rental form, which interrupted the flow and purpose of the beginner rental process. The program needed to be streamlined, intuitive, and separate from the beginner rental workflow.

Objective

In an early discussion with the company owner, he expressed his vision for expanding the professional instrument rental program. He aimed to establish a user-friendly system similar to the beginner rental program, explicitly targeting college-level and professional musicians.

My challenge was to design and implement a user-centric solution that addressed the current system's shortcomings while preserving the integrity of the beginner rental process.

Solution

Challenges Identified

  • The beginner rental form was repurposed for professional rentals, leading to user confusion and detracting from the beginner rental experience.
  • Essential user inputs such as school district and school were irrelevant for professional rentals but still required by the current form.
  • The Professional Instrument Rental Program needed to appear as a standalone offering but also utilize the same form as the beginner rental program

Proposed Solution

I created a dedicated webpage for the professional rental program, prioritizing clarity, usability, and a visually appealing design.

Landing Page Design
  • A visually striking banner featuring detailed imagery of professional instruments (flute, clarinet, and trumpet) and the tagline: "Experience the Difference."
  • A brief description of the professional rental program.
  • Prominent buttons to log in or create an account, essential for the application process.
Instrument Cards
  • Instruments were organized into categories, with each instrument displayed as a card.
  • Each card included:
    • A high-resolution image (sourced from manufacturers).
    • A brief description and key specifications of the instrument.
    • A call-to-action button labeled with the rental price and linked directly to a pre-populated rental form.
Streamlined Application Process
  • Clicking the CTA button directed users to a simplified rental form, pre-filled with the selected instrument, allowing users to complete the process with minimal steps.

Outcome

The redesigned program had a handful of successes, with several users renting professional instruments. However, significant barriers emerged post-launch:

Challenges Faced

  • Internal Resistance: Staff, particularly the credit department led by the owner's wife, expressed reluctance and frustration in processing professional rentals. This cultural resistance undermined the program's internal adoption.
  • Instrument Handling: Some instruments were returned in poor condition, leading to operational concerns about the viability of professional rentals.
  • Perception of Quality: Using stock images instead of actual photos of the instruments may have impacted the program's credibility and trust among potential renters.

Lessons Learned

  1. Stakeholder Alignment: Internal communication and buy-in from key stakeholders are critical. Future initiatives should involve all affected departments to address concerns proactively.
  2. User Trust: Incorporating actual images of the rental instruments could enhance credibility and allow better documentation of the instrument's condition pre-rental.
  3. Clear Terms: Establishing robust agreements regarding the care and handling of professional instruments would help mitigate risks and set renter expectations.

Conclusion

While the professional rental program demonstrated the potential to fill a niche in the market, its success was hampered by internal misalignment and operational challenges. This project underscored the importance of balancing user-centric design with organizational readiness and process optimization. The insights gained inform my approach to creating solutions considering user needs and organizational dynamics.

Trombone Day 22'

Background

Johnstonbaugh's Music Centers is primarily a school music dealer, serving educational institutions and students through instrument sales and rentals. However, its retail arm is a minor revenue contributor with significant growth potential. Johnstonbaugh's is known for its exclusive position as Western Pennsylvania's sole custom Shires Trombone Dealer, a reputation bolstered by its specialized trombone repair technicians, high-end inventory, and strong word-of-mouth in the trombone community.

Before the COVID-19 pandemic, Johnstonbaugh's hosted an annual promotional event called Trombone Day, which included performances, manufacturer visits, and merchandise sales. They canceled the event during the pandemic, but was reinstated in 2022 following my hiring as a Marketing and Website Specialist. My task was to organize, promote, and ensure the event's success while leveraging it to enhance the company's reputation and drive sales.

Objective

The goal was to relaunch Trombone Day as a well-attended event that engaged the trombone community, showcased Johnstonbaugh's expertise and inventory, and drove sales. Key success metrics included attendance, engagement, and event-related sales.

Solution

Research and Planning

  • Community Insights: I engaged with trombone forums, social media groups, and influencers.
  • Industry Connections: I attended the 2021 International Trombone Festival and networked with attendees.
  • Local Collaboration: I consulted with Pittsburgh-based trombonists to understand their needs and preferences.

This research shaped an event itinerary that catered to the community's interests while highlighting Johnstonbaugh's unique offerings.

Event Design

  • Manufacturer Representatives: Representatives from five leading trombone manufacturers showcased their products.
  • Masterclasses and Performances:
    • A masterclass by a symphony trombonist.
    • A performance by a university trombone choir.
    • A jazz masterclass by a renowned local professional.
  • Interactive Components: Opportunities for attendees to engage with experts and test high-end instruments.

Marketing Strategy

  • Custom Webpage:
    • An attention-grabbing headline and registration button.
    • A detailed schedule with images of key presenters.
    • Embedded video content from the jazz masterclass host to inspire interest.
    • Vendor highlights displayed as cards with logos, descriptions, and promotional videos.
  • Social Media and Email Marketing:
    • Regular promotional posts and event reminders.
    • Educational interviews with masterclass educators were shared via the company newsletter and social media.

      • Jeff Dee Advocate Interview Emmit Goods Advocate Interview

    • Personal email invitations to educators and influencers.
  • Paid Advertising: A Facebook ad campaign launched shortly before the event.

Outcome

The event successfully showcased Johnstonbaugh's expertise and cemented its reputation as a trusted dealer of professional trombones. However, attendance fell short of expectations, which limited sales potential.

Challenges Identified

  • Timing Conflict: The event coincided with a large regional band competition, reducing attendance from high school students, a key demographic for purchasing professional trombones.
  • Delayed Paid Advertising: While impactful, the late launch of Facebook ads limited the campaign's reach.
  • Underutilized Content: Lengthy, high-quality interviews with educators could have been shared with music publications and directly with other educators to extend promotion and engagement.
  • Pandemic Concerns: Lingering COVID-19 fears may have discouraged attendance at a public event.

Takeaways and Lessons Learned

  1. Invest in Paid Advertising Early: Paid social media ads proved highly effective and should have been part of the initial marketing strategy.
  2. Strategic Scheduling: Avoiding conflicts with significant events like band competitions is critical to maximizing attendance.
  3. Leverage Content Across Channels: Sharing interviews and rich content with external publications and educators would have boosted awareness.
  4. Audience Insights: Understanding and addressing pandemic-related hesitations (e.g., hybrid or virtual event options) might have increased participation.

Conclusion

Trombone Day 2022 successfully highlighted Johnstonbaugh's expertise and solidified its standing in the trombone community. While the event operated smoothly and was well-received by attendees, timing and marketing execution limited the event's full potential. Future events can build on these lessons to increase awareness, attendance, and sales.

MEAD

Background

Johnstonbaugh's Music Centers, a school music dealer, is a vital resource for educational institutions in Western Pennsylvania. Music educators are key clients, as they influence supply purchases for their programs and guide their students in renting instruments. With student rentals lasting 12–36 months and generating $25–$100 per month, the revenue potential tied to these educators is substantial.

The Pennsylvania Music Educators Association (PMEA) annual conference is the state's premier gathering of music educators. It features workshops, competitions, and a tradeshow for vendors like Johnstonbaugh's. Recognizing an opportunity to build deeper relationships with educators, I proposed hosting a Music Educators Appreciation Dinner the evening before the 2024 PMEA Conference.

Objective

The goal was to host a memorable event that:

  • Fostered goodwill and strengthened relationships with existing customers.
  • Introduced Johnstonbaugh's to potential clients in a friendly, non-sales setting.
  • Differentiated the company from competitors by offering an exclusive, high-quality experience.

Solution

Event Design

The Music Educators Appreciation Dinner was designed to be an engaging and professional affair:

  • Venue Selection: While the recommended venue was modest, it offered quality food, reasonable pricing, and proximity to the conference hotel. Johnstonbaugh's also purchased a shuttle service to accommodate attendees wishing to drink.
  • Entertainment:
    • Performances by the President's Brass Quartet, comprising former PMEA presidents.
    • A Johnstonbaugh's employee was also a professional pianist who played light music during the dinner.
  • Catering: A curated menu selected by the company owner and his wife.

Branding and Invitations

To establish a polished and cohesive theme:

  • Logo Design: A clean, formal logo featuring a lyre (a symbol of music education) was created in Adobe InDesign.
  • Print Invitations: Invitations were printed on elegant, natural-colored paper with black ink for a minimalist aesthetic. Each invitation included a QR code linking to a dedicated webpage for RSVPs.
  • Webpage Design: The mobile- and desktop-responsive webpage featured:
    • Event details.
    • An embedded map to the venue.
    • Titles and images of the evening's performers.
    • A Microsoft 365 embedded RSVP form.

Marketing and Outreach

  • Database Cleanup: Teacher addresses were updated by cross-referencing information from the Pennsylvania Department of Education's website.
  • Invitation Distribution:
    • Print invitations were mailed to over 300 teachers four weeks before the event.
    • Sales representatives followed up with personalized emails one week later, attaching a digital version of the invitation.
  • Follow-Up Communication: All RSVPs before the event were sent a confirmation email, including shuttle pickup details.

Outcome

The event was well-received by attendees, with 30–40 of the invited educators participating. The evening featured plentiful food and drink, engaging entertainment, and meaningful conversations. Relationships between educators and Johnstonbaugh's staff were visibly strengthened.

Challenges and Lessons Learned

Challenges

  • Limited Outreach to Prospects: The owner did not identify potential clients to invite, limiting the event's impact to existing relationships.
  • Low Shuttle Usage: Only two attendees used the shuttle, making it an unnecessary expense.
  • Missed Follow-Up Opportunities:
    • No post-event survey was conducted to gather feedback or further nurture relationships.
    • Prospects who did not attend were not contacted to understand their reasons.
  • Missed Publicity: While keeping the event discreet to avoid alerting competitors, a private email to other customers and prospects highlighting its success could have generated goodwill without compromising its exclusivity.

Takeaways

  1. Prospect Engagement: Including potential clients in the invitation list is essential for expanding influence and generating new business.
  2. Post-Event Strategy: Surveys and follow-ups are critical to maximizing the impact of events.
  3. Cost Management: Carefully evaluate additional services, such as shuttles, based on expected usage.
  4. Strategic Publicity: Highlighting event success can reinforce credibility and reputation among broader audiences without compromising competitive advantage.

Conclusion

The Music Educators Appreciation Dinner was a successful initiative that deepened existing client relationships and showcased Johnstonbaugh's commitment to the music education community. While future iterations could benefit from refined strategies in outreach and follow-up, this event demonstrated the value of creating meaningful, in-person experiences for key stakeholders.
